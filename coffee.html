Coffee Taster's Flavor Wheel

The Coffee Taster's Flavor Wheel is a tool used by coffee professionals to describe and categorize the various flavors and aromas present in coffee. It was developed by the Specialty Coffee Association (SCA) in collaboration with World Coffee Research to provide a standardized vocabulary for discussing coffee flavor profiles.

The Flavor Wheel is a circular diagram that organizes coffee flavors into different categories and subcategories. At the center of the wheel, there are broad categories such as "sweet" and "sour," which represent the basic taste attributes of coffee. As you move outwards, the wheel becomes more detailed, with more specific flavors and aromas. The outer rim of the Flavor Wheel contains descriptors for complex and specific flavors that can be found in coffee, such as "floral," "citrus," "caramel," "chocolate," "nutty," "spicy," and many more. These descriptors help tasters articulate and communicate the nuanced characteristics of different coffees.

The Coffee Taster's Flavor Wheel is a valuable tool for coffee professionals, including tasters, roasters, and baristas, as it facilitates discussions about coffee flavors and assists in quality control, sourcing decisions, and overall appreciation of the beverage. It enables tasters to communicate effectively and consistently about the sensory experiences associated with different coffees.

Pour Over Coffee

There is a video on the right show how to make Pour Over Coffee with "V60":

Pour-over coffee is a brewing method that involves manually pouring hot water over coffee grounds to extract the flavors and aromas. It's a popular technique known for its simplicity and ability to produce a clean, flavorful cup of coffee.

  1. Grind your coffee
  2. Set up the equipment
  3. Pre-wet the filter
  4. Add coffee grounds
  5. Bloom the coffee
  6. Pour in a controlled manner
  7. Wait and repeat
  8. Serve and enjoy
